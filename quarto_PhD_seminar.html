The Standard Model

The most successful theory (so far) of fundamental interactions

Gauge principle:
Interactions arise by requiring invariance under local symmetry transformations.

  • Electromagnetic â†’ \(\mathrm{U}(1)_{\text{em}}\)
    acts on charged particles
  • Weak â†’ \(\mathrm{SU}(2)_L\)
    responsable of radioactive decays
  • Strong â†’ \(\mathrm{SU}(3)_C\)
    binds quarks inside hadrons

\[\mathcal{L}_{\text{SM}} \;=\; \mathcal{L}_{\text{gauge}} + \mathcal{L}_{\text{matter}} + \mathcal{L}_{\text{Higgs}}\]

Gravity unfortunately is not included in the Standard Model.

Chiral Limit in QCD

Chiral Transformations:

\[\psi \;\longrightarrow\; e^{\,i \alpha \gamma_5/2}\, \psi;\quad \quad \bar{\psi} \;\longrightarrow\; \bar{\psi}\, e^{\,i \alpha \gamma_5/2}\]

in the \(\small m_f \to 0\) is a symmetry of the Lagrangian

  • Left / Right modes:
    \[ \psi_L = P_L \psi, \quad \psi_R = P_R \psi \] with \(P_{L,R} = \frac{1\mp\gamma_5}{2}\)
  • \[ \bar{\psi}\, i\gamma^\mu D_\mu \psi = \bar{\psi}_\textcolor{red}{\small L} i\gamma^\mu D_\mu \psi_\textcolor{red}{\small L} + \bar{\psi}_\textcolor{blue}{\small R} i\gamma^\mu D_\mu \psi_\textcolor{blue}{\small R} \]

    â†’ does not mix chirality

  • \[ \,\bar{\psi}m\psi = \bar{\psi}_\textcolor{red}{\small L} m \psi_\textcolor{blue}{\small R} + \bar{\psi}_\textcolor{blue}{\small R} m \psi_\textcolor{red}{\small L} \]

    â†’ explicitly couples \(\textcolor{red}{\small L}\) and \(\textcolor{blue}{\small R}\)

  • Chiral limit: \(\small m_u = m_d = 0\) â‡’ \(\small \mathrm{SU}(2)_V \times \mathrm{SU}(2)_A \times \mathrm{U}(1)_V\) is a global symmetry:
    • QCD spectrum does not show parity doubling.

Chiral symmetry breaking in real QCD

  1. Spontaneous breaking
    • \(\small \langle \bar{\psi}\psi \rangle \neq 0\) (non-trivial QCD vacuum)
    • \(\small \mathrm{SU}(2)_V \times \mathrm{SU}(2)_A \times \mathrm{U}(1)_V \;\;\longrightarrow\;\; \mathrm{SU}(2)_V \times \mathrm{U}(1)_V\)
    • 3 Nambuâ€“Goldstone bosons â‡’ the pions
  2. Explicit breaking
    • light quark masses \(\small m_{u,d} \neq 0\)
    • GMOR: \(\small{ M_{\pi}^2 = (m_u + m_d)}\frac{\large \langle \bar{\psi}\psi \rangle}{\large f_{\pi}^2} \simeq 135 \mathrm{\, MeV}\)

Interactions in QCD

Gauge invariance not only dictates the kinetic terms, but also fixes the interaction structure:

\(\small \sim g \bar{\psi}\psi A\)

\(\small \sim g \partial A A A \quad \quad \quad \sim g^2 AAAA\)

  • QED-like vertex
    • quarkâ€“gluon interaction
    • analogous to electronâ€“photon in QED
  • New in QCD
    • 3-gluon and 4-gluon self-interactions
    • consequence of non-abelian \(\mathrm{SU}(3)_C\)
    • lead to confinement, asymptotic freedom, glueballsâ€¦

Quantum corrections lead to divergencies:
Interactions are treated in the renormalization framework â†’ running of the coupling \(g(\mu)\).

Running of the Coupling

  • Scale dependent coupling: \(\small g \,\longrightarrow\, g(\mu)\)
  • Governed by the Î²-function:
    \[\small \mu \frac{dg}{d\mu} = \beta(g), \quad \beta(g) = -\beta_0 \frac{g^3}{16\pi^2} + \mathcal{O}(g^5) \] with \(\small \;\beta_0 = 11 - \tfrac{2}{3} N_f\)
  • Solution at 1-loop:
    \[\small \alpha_s(\mu) \equiv \frac{g^2(\mu)}{4\pi} = \frac{1}{\beta_0 \ln(\mu^2 / \Lambda_{\text{QCD}}^2)} \]

  • Consequences:
    • \(\small \mu \to \infty \;\;\Rightarrow\;\; \alpha_s(\mu) \to 0 \;\Rightarrow\; \color{#EB811B}\text{asymptotic freedom}\)
    • \(\small \mu \to \Lambda_{\text{QCD}} \;\;\Rightarrow\;\; \alpha_s(\mu) \to \infty \;\Rightarrow\; \color{#EB811B}\text{confinement}\)

QCD becomes strongly coupled in the IR â†’ non-perturbative tools are required!

Confinement

  • As \(\small \alpha_s(\mu) \to \infty\) at low energies â†’ quarks and gluons cannot propagate as free states
  • QCD potential:

    \[\small V(r) = -\,\frac{4}{3}\frac{\alpha_s}{r} + \sigma r \quad \quad \quad \;\sigma \approx 0.9\ \mathrm{GeV/fm} \]

  • Force:

    \[ \small |dV/dr| \approx 0.9 \times 1.6\cdot10^{-10} \text{J}/10^{-15}\ \text{m} \approx 1.6\cdot10^{4}\ \text{N} \]

  • Only color-singlet bound states are physical:
    • mesons (\(q\bar q\))
    • baryons (\(qqq\))
    • glueballs (\(gg, ggg,\dots\))
  • No direct experimental evidence of isolated quarks
    • jet formation in high-energy collisions
    • linear potential at large distances (\(V(r) \sim \sigma r\))
